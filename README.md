# Day 7 – Claude Model Selection & Reasoning Effort

The goal of Day 7 was to understand how choosing different Claude models and reasoning effort levels affects the quality, depth, speed, and usefulness of AI-generated outputs.

Instead of treating Claude as a single AI model, I explored how different models and effort settings can be used strategically for different tasks.

## 💡 Biggest Insight

The most valuable insight was that choosing the right model is often just as important as writing the right prompt.

The same task can produce very different results depending on which model and effort level is used.

---

## 📚 What I Learned About Model Selection

Before this challenge, I focused mostly on prompt writing.

Through this experiment, I learned that:

* Different models serve different purposes
* Not every task requires the most powerful model
* Reasoning effort influences output quality
* Strategic model selection improves productivity

This helped me understand how to use Claude more intentionally rather than relying on one model for everything.

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1. Every Model Has a Purpose

Haiku, Sonnet, and Opus are designed for different levels of complexity.

### 2. Sonnet Is the Best Daily Driver

For learning, coding, and productivity, Sonnet provides the best balance between speed and quality.

### 3. Deep Reasoning Requires More Effort

High and Max effort settings are valuable for research, planning, and complex problem-solving.

### 4. Speed Is Not Always the Goal

The fastest answer is not always the most useful answer.

### 5. AI Usage Should Be Intentional

Choosing the right model can improve both efficiency and output quality.
